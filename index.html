MANAGE E-WASTE PROPERLY

E-waste is the fastest-growing solid waste globally, with 53.6 million tonnes produced in 2019, but only 17.4% recycled. Hazardous substances like lead are released through improper disposal methods. E-waste recycling poses health risks, especially to children and pregnant women, with millions of informal workers, including women and children, at risk of exposure, according to ILO and WHO.

THE PROBLEMS OF
E-WASTE

Environmental Impact

E-waste contains harmful chemicals like lead and mercury that can contaminate soil and water. When burned, it releases toxic gases that contribute to air pollution and climate change.

Human Health Risks

In developing countries, e-waste is often dismantled without proper safety, exposing workers to dangerous toxins. This leads to severe health problems like respiratory issues and nerve damage.

A Global Problem

E-waste is often exported to poorer countries, worsening environmental and health issues in already vulnerable communities.

Rapid Technological Advancements

The fast pace of innovation in electronics leads to frequent upgrades. Consumers replace devices like smartphones and laptops regularly, contributing to the rise of e-waste as older models become obsolete.

THE CAUSES OF E-WASTE

Short Product Lifecycles

Manufacturers design products with shorter lifespans, encouraging consumers to buy newer versions frequently. This practice, known as planned obsolescence, increases the amount of discarded electronics.

Lack of Proper Recycling Infrastructure

In many parts of the world, there is inadequate infrastructure for recycling electronic devices. As a result, most e-waste ends up in landfills or is improperly handled, worsening the global e-waste problem.

THE SOLUTION

Circular Economy

Focus: Design electronics for longer use, repair, and recycling.
Actions: Encourage repair, refurbishment, and efficient recycling to reduce waste and recover valuable materials.

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)

Focus: Hold manufacturers accountable for the full lifecycle of their products.
Actions: Mandate companies to take back old devices for recycling and adopt eco-friendly production practices.

Consumer Awareness

Focus: Educate consumers on e-waste impacts and proper disposal.
Actions: Promote recycling, set up collection points, and encourage responsible consumption.
